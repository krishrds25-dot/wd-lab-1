Tribute to Atal Bihari Vajpayee

Atal Bihari Vajpayee was an Indian poet, writer and statesman who served as the prime minister of India, first for a term of 13 days in 1996, then for 6 years from 1998 to 2004. He was the first non-Congress prime minister to serve a full term in the office

Achievements
  • Birth & Education: Born in Gwalior (1924), he earned an M.A. in Political Science.
  • RSS & Media: Joined the RSS in 1939 and edited several Hindi publications, including Panchjanya.
  • Parliamentary Record: Served as a Member of Parliament across nine Lok Sabha terms, starting in 1957.
  • Foreign Minister: Served as Minister of External Affairs (1977–1979) and was the first to speak in Hindi at the UN General Assembly.
  • Nuclear & Military: Conducted the Pokhran-II tests (1998) and led India to victory in the 1999 Kargil War.
  • Infrastructure: Launched the Golden Quadrilateral project for highways and the PMGSY for rural roads.
